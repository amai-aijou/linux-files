			*       Hyperbolic Discounting        *
			*                aka                  *
			* "They should Teach This In Schools" *
			*                                     *
			***************************************

Oftentimes, when a person notices a necessary behavioral change, but isn't prepared for the work it takes to achieve their goal, they'll wish for the habits to have been magically beamed into their head in the distant past.

A close cousin of Hyperbolic Discounting, I call this the "They Should Teach This In Schools" fallacy. Since they no longer feel the emotions of their former self's labor, they see the past as "free", with no cost in time or energy.

It allows them to shift blame, either to the past self, or worse, to those around them, whilst simultaneously providing the perfect excuse to continue the negative behavior. However, neglecting the needed adjustment (and allowing a decoupling of the temporal selves) is rife with hidden consequences.

Ten years from now, today will be the "back then" they speak about. Since they view their future self as an entirely different person, they pay little mind to that person's wants or needs.

This mentality is prevalent in investing. They'll say, 'I should've bought stock in Company XYZ in 2006...I'd be rich!". What they don't tell you is they'll be saying the same thing twenty years from now about today.
